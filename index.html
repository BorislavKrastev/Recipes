About Bulgarian Cuisine

Bulgarian cuisine is a melting pot of flavors, influenced by its geographical location at the crossroads of Europe and Asia. It shares characteristics with Balkan, Turkish, and Greek cuisines but maintains its unique identity.

Key ingredients include fresh vegetables (especially tomatoes, cucumbers, peppers), dairy products like yogurt and sirene (white brine cheese), and a variety of meats such as pork, lamb, and chicken. Dill, savory, and paprika are common spices.

Meals are often hearty, rich in flavor, and perfect for sharing. Bulgarian hospitality ensures that no guest leaves the table hungry!
